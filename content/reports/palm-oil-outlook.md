## The Setup

A month ago, B50 was a rumor. Today it is policy.

On April 1, Indonesia confirmed mandatory B50 biodiesel starting July 1, 2026. Coordinating Minister Airlangga Hartarto tied the decision directly to the Middle East conflict and rising crude prices. The program will cut fossil fuel consumption by 4 million kiloliters per year. Pertamina is ready to blend. The market rallied 18.75% in March on the anticipation alone.

FCPO closed at MYR 4,839 on April 3. The 52-week high is MYR 4,918. Indonesia's April CPO reference price came in at USD 989.63/MT, up 5.4% from March.

The question is no longer whether B50 happens. It is whether the supply side can absorb it.

## The Balance Sheet

![CPO Supply-Demand Balance](/images/palm-oil-supply-demand.png)

USDA projects global palm oil production at 80.7 MT for 2025/26. Indonesia contributes 46.7 MT, Malaysia 19.6 MT, and the rest of the world 14.4 MT.

On paper, production covers demand. USDA's baseline global consumption sits at 77.7 MT. But that number was set before B50 was confirmed. Adjusting for the incremental 1.5 MT of CPO that B50 pulls into Indonesia's domestic biodiesel program in H2, total consumption rises to 79.2 MT. The surplus narrows to 1.5 MT.

That is 1.5 MT on an 80 MT market. Essentially nothing.

## Three things make even that thin cushion unreliable.

**First, Indonesia's supply is not as secure as the headline suggests.** The government has seized 3.3 million hectares of plantation land. 1.5 million hectares already went to the state-owned PT Agrinas. Another 1.8 million hectares are under verification. Fastmarkets estimates 2 to 5 MT of production is at risk. State-managed plantations historically see productivity drops of 20 to 30% during ownership transitions. If even the low end of that materializes, 1 MT disappears from the supply side and the surplus flips to deficit.

**Second, Malaysia cannot fill the gap.** MPOB projects 19.6 MT for 2026, down 400,000 tonnes from 2025. Trees are resting after strong Q4 2025 output. Aging plantations are expanding to 2 million hectares by 2027. Planted area is stagnating at 5.6 million hectares. Labour shortages are structural. There is no production upside from Malaysia.

**Third, the demand side keeps tightening.** India's palm imports surged 51% in January. Palm trades at a USD 100+ discount to soy. MPOC projects Indian consumption up 800,000 tonnes this year. Malaysian palm exports jumped 44 to 57% month-on-month in March. The only drag is China, where buyers are switching to soy and canola.

The math is simple. Supply is flat at best and fragile at worst. Demand just got a structural boost from B50. The surplus is razor-thin before any negative surprise.

## What B50 Actually Means for the Market

Under B40, Indonesia's biodiesel program absorbed roughly 12 MT of CPO per year. GAPKI estimates full-year B50 would push that to 16 MT. APROBI projects 17 to 18 MT. Since B50 starts July 1, the blended full-year pull is approximately 13.3 MT for biodiesel. Combined with food and oleochemical demand of roughly 10 MT, total Indonesia domestic consumption reaches 23.3 MT.

Indonesia produces 46.7 MT but now consumes 23.3 MT at home. That leaves 23.4 MT for export. In 2025, Indonesia exported over 28 MT. The exportable surplus is shrinking by roughly 5 MT. For a market that sets the global price, that is a significant structural shift.

Every tonne diverted to biodiesel is a tonne removed from the export market. India, the EU, and China do not have a substitute at scale. Soy and canola cannot absorb the shortfall without their own prices rising. The tightness in palm does not stay contained. It spreads across the vegetable oil complex.

## My Call

![Forecast](/images/palm-oil-drivers.png)

**I am bullish. FCPO breaks MYR 5,000 by Q3 2026. Six-month range: MYR 4,750 to 5,050.**

**April to June:** The market consolidates between MYR 4,750 and 4,950. MPOB March data drops April 10. If stocks come in below 1.6 MT, prices push toward the upper end. Crude oil stays supported above USD 90 on Middle East tensions. The floor holds. This is the accumulation window.

**July to September:** B50 goes live. Indonesia's biodiesel program starts pulling an additional 3 to 4 million kiloliters of CPO-based FAME per year. Seasonal production picks up, but the question is whether Indonesian output can simultaneously feed a 50% biodiesel blend and maintain export volumes at anywhere near 2025 levels. I do not think it can. Physical tightness shows up in basis and premiums first, then in the futures curve. FCPO tests MYR 5,000 and holds above it.

**What breaks the thesis:** B50 gets rolled back to B45 because the infrastructure cannot handle it. Indonesian production normalizes faster than expected. Crude drops below USD 70 on a Middle East ceasefire, undermining biodiesel economics. All three would need to happen simultaneously for prices to pull back below MYR 4,750. Possible but improbable under the current geopolitical backdrop.
